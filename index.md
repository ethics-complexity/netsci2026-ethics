## Why this satellite matters {#description}

Network science studies systems of interconnected entities whose collective behavior often emerges in complex, adaptive, and difficult-to-predict ways. As the field increasingly engages with data-rich, socially embedded, and policy-relevant domains, it also raises ethical questions that do not always fit neatly within traditional frameworks.

Questions of distributed responsibility, emergent harm, data sensitivity, intervention, fairness, and downstream societal impact are becoming central to how network science is practiced. This satellite creates a dedicated space for the community to reflect on those questions together.

Through keynote talks, an interactive panel, breakout activities, and short community contributions, participants will share experiences, identify ethical challenges, and explore concrete ways to foster responsible and socially informed network science.
